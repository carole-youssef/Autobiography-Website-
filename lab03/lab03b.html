Romania is an exquisite and captivating country located at the heart of Southeastern and Central Europe. Covering an area of 92,043 square miles, Romania is home to stunning landscapes, from the awe-inspiring Carpathian Mountains to the serene coastline of the Black Sea. Its capital city, Bucharest, is the largest city, with a population of around 2.15 million people. The primary religion in Romania is Christian Orthodoxy, encompassing around 81% of the population.

Romanian Traditions

  • During Christmas and Easter, traditional folklore customs such as plugușorul and sorcova are practised.
  • On March 1st, women are gifted with a mărțișor, a type of tailsman given as a sign of good luck.
  • Romanian cuisine is influenced by German, Austrian, Greek, Bulgarian and Serbian flavours.
  • Traditional dishes such as chiftele, tobă and tochitură are eaten during Christmas time.
  • The traditional alcoholic beverage is Țuică, a strong plum-infused brandy.

All About Sports

The most popular sport in Romania is Football, with over 219,000 players registered as of 2018. The Romanian national team played their first match in 1922 and is one of the only teams that have played in the first three FIFA World Cups. The best club in Romania is Steaua București, who became the first Eastern European team to win the UEFA Champions League in 1986. Some famous footballers include Dudu Georgescu, Adrian Mutu and Cosmin Contra.

Tennis is the second most popular sport in Romania, with the country reaching the Davis Cup finals three times in 1969, 1971, and 1972. Ilie Năstase is one of the most famous tennis players, and was the first tennis player to end the year as World Number 1 in the ATP rankings in 1973. Other famous tennis players include Simona Halep and Horia Tecău.
